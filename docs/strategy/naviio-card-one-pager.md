# Naviio Card — one-page brief

*Rough planning numbers, June 2026. Ranges, not promises — validate with a card partner before quoting.*

## The one-liner
**An AI CFO that swipes.** A business charge card where every purchase is also a piece of advice from Naviio — categorized, benchmarked, and policy-checked in real time.

## Who it's for
Small and mid-sized businesses (starting with a focused vertical, e.g. med-spas / clinics / agencies) that want spending control *and* a finance brain, without hiring a CFO.

## What it is
- A business **charge card** plus instant **virtual cards** for employees and subscriptions.
- Every swipe lands in Naviio in seconds — already categorized, already in the P&L.
- **Navi (the AI CFO) watches each transaction live:** warns before you overspend, flags "you're paying more than businesses your size," catches duplicates/fraud, and enforces plain-English spending rules.
- Pay bills (AP) from the same place.

## How it makes money
The card is **free to the customer.** Naviio earns on the flow:

1. **Interchange** — a small cut of every swipe (the main engine). Business-card interchange is high; after paying the card partners, Naviio nets roughly **1–2% of card spend**.
2. **Bill-pay fees** — small per-payment fees on ACH/wires.
3. **Treasury** — the spread on idle cash held in the account.
4. **Software subscription** — the existing Naviio plan/credits, on top.

### What that's worth per customer, per year

Assuming Naviio nets ~**1.5%** of card spend (middle of the range):

| Customer card spend / yr | Interchange to Naviio / yr |
|---|---|
| $240k ($20k/mo — small) | ~$3,600 |
| $600k ($50k/mo — typical) | ~$9,000 |
| $1.2M ($100k/mo — larger) | ~$18,000 |

For comparison, a software-only subscription is maybe ~$1,200/yr. **The card can be 3–15× the revenue per customer** — same customer, far more value, and it funds making the product free.

### What that's worth as you scale (typical customer, ~$9k/yr each)

| Customers | Interchange / yr |
|---|---|
| 1,000 | ~$9M |
| 10,000 | ~$90M |
| 50,000 | ~$450M |

(For context, Ramp reached ~$1.5B revenue at ~70,000 customers on the same engine.)

**Costs to net against this:** card-program fees, fraud losses, support, and any cashback/rewards you offer. Net margin is lower than gross interchange — but the data you collect is the real prize.

## Why it wins (and doesn't just copy Ramp)
- **Advisor-first, not spreadsheet-first.** Ramp's card records spending; Naviio's card *coaches* it. Every purchase is a Navi insight.
- **Vertical depth.** A card that understands a med-spa's economics (equipment ROI, treatment margins) — something a horizontal giant won't build for every niche.
- **The data snowball.** Every swipe makes Naviio's benchmarks and advice smarter, which saves customers more, which brings more spend onto the card. The same loop that made Ramp worth $44B.

## What it takes to build (you don't build a bank)
Partner for the money plumbing; build the brain on top.
- **Issuer-processor** (Lithic, Stripe Issuing, or Marqeta) — creates and runs the cards.
- **Sponsor bank** — sits behind the processor.
- **KYB / compliance** — verify each business is real (usually bundled with the issuer).
- **Naviio builds:** the AI CFO, live categorization, benchmarks, spend rules, the app.

Rough path: pick the partner and vertical → pilot with ~25–50 existing customers → expand. Plan for the compliance and risk work that comes with moving money (it's the cost of the moat).

## The headline
Naviio already owns the hard, rare part — the **AI CFO**. Adding the card turns it from a tool you pay for into a business that **earns on every transaction and gets smarter with each one** — the exact flywheel behind the biggest fintech of the moment, aimed where they're not looking.
